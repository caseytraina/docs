> V2 brings Overlap into one connected workspace for creating clips, polishing videos, scheduling social posts, and tracking what happens after publishing.

<Info>
  This page is unlisted. Share the direct link when you want to give someone a guided overview of V2.
</Info>

## The big picture

Overlap V2 is built around a simpler loop:

1. Bring in content from a workflow, upload, or connected source.
2. Let Overlap create or prepare clips.
3. Polish the result in Studio.
4. Schedule or publish across connected social accounts.
5. Review performance from the same workspace.

The new experience also cleans up navigation. The sidebar now centers the core product areas: `Home`, `Workflows`, `Posting Personas`, `Editing Agent`, `Library`, `Brand Kits`, `Social Calendar`, `Analytics`, and `Accounts`.

## Home is now an operating dashboard

The V2 home screen gives you a fast read on what is happening across the workspace.

From `Home`, you can:

- Review `Today at a glance` performance with a views trend
- Open recent projects and generated clips
- See outgoing posts without jumping into the calendar first
- Start a new project from `New`
- Choose `Run a workflow` when you want automation to handle the next job
- Choose `Edit a video from scratch` when you want to upload and cut manually
- Open the Overlap 2.0 guided tour from the sidebar

Projects now carry more context, including recent activity and clip counts, so it is easier to tell which source content is already producing useful outputs.

## Workflows are clearer and more powerful

Workflows are the V2 automation layer. They turn repeatable content operations into a visible trigger, editing, and export flow.

<Frame>
  ![Overlap V2 workflows list](/images/generated/beta-workflows-framed.png)
</Frame>

In V2, the workflows page supports:

- Search across workflow names
- `List` and `Cards` views
- Bulk selection with `Select`
- Statuses such as `Listening` and `Off`
- Last-run visibility
- Trigger and export summaries
- Workflow tags for organization
- Draft saving before publishing

The workflow builder also adds and improves nodes across the pipeline, including trigger nodes, subtitle styling, title overlays, audiograms, B-roll, color looks, and cleanup nodes such as `Remove Fluff`.

## Studio has a full timeline editor

Studio is the hands-on editor for a specific clip or blank project. V2 brings the preview, transcript, layered timeline, and finishing tools into one editing surface.

<Frame>
  ![Overlap V2 Studio editor](/images/generated/beta-studio-framed.png)
</Frame>

The new Studio experience includes:

- A layered timeline for `Rich Text`, `Subtitles`, `Watermark`, video, and media items
- Faster scrubbing, seeking, and playback behavior
- Timeline zoom, fit controls, and speed control
- Dragging, trimming, and multi-select timeline operations
- Rich text title overlays and reusable title styling
- Subtitle editing with improved style controls
- B-roll, image, media, and color controls
- Reframe access from the editor
- Export and share actions from the top bar

You can start Studio from an existing clip with `Edit Clip`, or create a blank project from `Home` with `Edit a video from scratch`.

<Tip>
  Some older clips and workflows may still use legacy names like `Clipping Agent`. They still work in the V2 workspace.
</Tip>

## Transcript editing and AI cleanup are faster

Studio's transcript tools are designed for content-aware edits. Instead of finding every cut visually, you can highlight words or phrases and apply the right action.

V2 supports workflows such as:

- Cutting spoken sections from the video
- Muting selected transcript ranges
- Editing captions without changing the video timing
- Removing filler words
- Removing stutter words
- Removing curse words
- Removing silences
- Removing punctuation
- Highlighting keywords

The `AI Tools` panel also collects finishing actions such as `End Card`, `Intro Music`, `Outro Music`, and `Add Speaker Cards`.

## The Library is built for real clip operations

The V2 `Library` is more than a grid of clips. It is where teams can search, filter, select, tag, share, and turn clips into posts.

Library improvements include:

- Faster clip search
- Filters for `Tags`, `Status`, `People`, `Keywords`, `Workflow`, `Aspect Ratio`, and `Duration`
- `Grid` and `List` views
- Bulk selection across clips
- Bulk tag creation
- Bulk delete
- Bulk download
- `Create Share Link` for selected clips
- `Create Social Posts` from selected clips
- Episode-level clip organization and bulk tagging

V2 also adds stronger support for media libraries and connected asset sources, including Iconik-enabled library views when available.

## Social Calendar is the publishing command center

`Social Calendar` is where scheduled, posted, pending, and manually-created posts come together.

<Frame>
  ![Overlap V2 Social Calendar](/images/generated/beta-social-calendar-current-framed.png)
</Frame>

The V2 calendar supports:

- `Week` and `Month` layouts
- Calendar and list-style views
- `Filters` for narrowing posts
- `Manage` actions for bulk schedule work
- `Create Post` for manual scheduling
- Post detail sheets for last-mile edits
- Account and platform grouping
- Approval-aware scheduled posts
- Mention handling for social captions
- Safer scheduled-time handling

Use the calendar when you need to review the publishing queue, adjust schedule spacing, approve posts, or create a post without waiting for a workflow.

## Posting is broader across platforms

V2 expands the posting layer so workflows, manual posts, and calendar edits can target more real-world social setups.

Highlights include:

- Instagram publishing improvements, including Reels grid controls
- Instagram collaborator support from workflows
- YouTube post details such as titles, descriptions, thumbnails, and playlist-related options
- TikTok support across social previews and analytics data
- Snapchat integration
- iHeart scheduling and direct-posting work
- Better account labels so display names and usernames are clearer
- Improved relinking when a social account needs attention

This makes Overlap more useful as an ongoing publishing system, not only a clip-generation tool.

## Analytics connect content to outcomes

V2 brings performance into the daily workspace instead of isolating it from the creative flow.

Analytics improvements include:

- The `Today at a glance` chart on `Home`
- A full `Analytics` page for workspace performance
- Platform analytics across connected social accounts
- Account-level platform rows
- Historical platform graphing
- All-time views and range-based reporting
- Post-level analytics views
- Cleaner handling for connected account metrics

The goal is simple: see what is working, then use that signal to decide what to make, edit, or schedule next.

## Team, billing, and enterprise controls are more complete

V2 also rounds out the workspace administration layer.

New and improved admin surfaces include:

- `Accounts` for connected social profiles
- Organization member controls
- Team and enterprise member pages
- Enterprise organization, usage, shared resource, clip, and post views
- `Billing and Usage` with subscription status
- Processing-hours usage
- Active workflow usage
- Pricing and cancellation controls

## Better performance across the product

A large part of V2 is the work you feel rather than the work you see.

Recent improvements include:

- Faster initial loading on home, calendar, clips, and overview pages
- Better caching for overview, scheduled posts, and source metadata
- Smoother Studio scrubbing and clip playback
- More stable frame-accurate timeline behavior
- More reliable exports from original source media
- Improved handling for audio-only and MP3 export cases
- Better diagnostics for browser and video playback performance

Those improvements are meant to make the product feel calmer during repeated daily use: fewer delays, fewer stale states, and more confidence when you edit or publish.
